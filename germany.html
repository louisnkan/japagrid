Best for Nigerians

Skilled Worker Visa (Fachkräfteeinwanderungsgesetz)

For people with a recognized vocational or university qualification and a concrete job offer in Germany. The most direct path for most Nigerian graduates.

Processing Time 6–12 weeks
Job Required First? Yes
Language B1 German (usually)
Salary Threshold €43,800+ (2026)

Job Seeker Visa

6-month visa to search for a job in Germany. No work allowed during the search period. Best for people with strong qualifications and savings.

Duration 6 months
Proof of Funds ~€11,000 blocked account
Degree Required Yes, recognized

Opportunity Card (Chancenkarte)

Points-based system for job seekers. Launched June 2024. No job offer required, but you need points from qualifications, experience, language, and age.

Duration 1 year (extendable)
Part-time Work Allowed (up to 20 hrs/week)
Points Needed 6+ (out of possible criteria)

Student Visa → Work

Study at a German university, work part-time, transition to skilled worker visa after graduation. Longer path but builds network and language.

Blocked Account ~€11,208/year
Work During Study 120 full days/year
Job Search Post-Grad 18 months

Job Market Reality

Germany has a genuine labor shortage, but "shortage" does not mean "easy for everyone." Language requirements are real, and employers prefer candidates already in Germany.

Software Engineering / IT Very High
Healthcare (Nursing, Caregiving) Very High
Engineering (Mechanical, Electrical) High
Skilled Trades (Plumbing, Electrical) High
Data Science / AI High
Humanities / Social Sciences Low

⚠️ Reality check: English-speaking jobs exist in tech, but most roles require B1–B2 German. The "Germany needs workers" headline is true for specific sectors, not universal.

Qualification Recognition

This is where most Nigerians get stuck. Your degree is not automatically valid in Germany. You must get it recognized — and the process varies wildly by profession and federal state.

University Degrees

Handled by anabin database. Check if your Nigerian university and degree are listed as "H+" (equivalent) or "H−" (not equivalent). If H−, you may need additional coursework or exams.

Where to Check anabin.kmk.org
Cost €100–€300
Time 2–6 months

Vocational / Trade Qualifications

Handled by the chambers of commerce (IHK/HWK) in each state. Recognition is patchy — some trades have streamlined processes, others require retraining in Germany.

Where to Apply State-specific IHK/HWK
Cost €100–€600
Time 3–12 months

⚠️ Critical: Start recognition before you apply for jobs. Many employers won't consider you without it. The "Make it in Germany" portal has a free pre-check tool.

The Integration Reality

Germany is not a "melting pot." Integration is formalized — language courses (B1 required for PR), integration courses, and civic knowledge tests. The system works if you engage with it, but passive English-speaking expat life is limited. You will need German for banking, housing, healthcare, and deep social connection. Start learning before you arrive.

⚠️

The Catch

What no one puts in the headline

  • Housing crisis is real. In major cities, finding an apartment takes months. Landlords demand SCHUFA (credit score you don't have), high deposits, and German-speaking references. Start searching before you arrive.
  • Taxes are high. That €55k salary becomes ~€32k net. Factor this into your planning. "High salary" in Germany is not what it sounds like pre-tax.
  • Bureaucracy is a lifestyle. Registration, tax ID, health insurance, bank account — each requires appointments, documents, and patience. The system works, but it moves at its own pace.
  • Language is non-negotiable for integration. You can survive in English in tech. You cannot thrive. B1 is the floor, not the ceiling.
  • Degree recognition is a gatekeeper. Many Nigerian engineering degrees are recognized, but the process is slow and state-dependent. Don't assume your qualification transfers cleanly.
