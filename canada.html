Canada

The "easy Canada" story died in 2024. What remains is a system that still works — but only if you understand the new rules. Express Entry scores have climbed into the stratosphere. The study permit cap is real. The housing crisis in Toronto and Vancouver is not a headline; it is a daily calculation. This is what relocation to Canada actually looks like in 2026.

Immigration

Visa Pathways

Express Entry is still the flagship program, but the math has shifted. General draws now demand 520–540+ CRS points — a threshold most Nigerian applicants without Canadian work experience or a provincial nomination cannot meet. The realistic routes in 2026 are category-based draws and PNPs.

Express Entry — General Draw
Very Competitive

The all-program draws that once hovered around 470–480 CRS have climbed to 520–540+. For a Nigerian applicant with a master's degree, strong IELTS scores, and three years of foreign work experience, the typical CRS lands around 470–490. Without Canadian work experience or a provincial nomination, you are likely below the cut-off.

The Numbers

Current cut-off: 520–540+ CRS. Processing: 6–8 months after ITA.

Category-Based Draws
Realistic Path

IRCC now targets specific occupations and skills. If you fall into one of these categories, your CRS requirement drops significantly. STEM and healthcare are the most relevant for Nigerian graduates; French proficiency is a dark horse that few exploit.

CRS Thresholds by Category

STEM: ~490–500. Healthcare: ~480–490. French proficiency: ~420–430. Trades: ~425–435.

Provincial Nominee Programs
+600 CRS Points

A provincial nomination adds 600 CRS points — effectively guaranteeing an ITA. Ontario, British Columbia, and Alberta are the most popular, but each province runs its own streams with distinct criteria. Some require a job offer; others do not. Research the specific stream, not just the province.

The Trade-Off

Processing adds 3–6 months to your Express Entry timeline. You must intend to live in the nominating province.

Reality Check

If your CRS is below 500 and you do not qualify for a category-based draw or PNP, do not waste money on an Express Entry profile. The pool is crowded and the cut-off is not coming down. Consider a study permit or provincial stream instead.

Education Route

Study Permit Changes

The study-to-PR pipeline was Canada's great equalizer. It still exists, but the gates have narrowed. A cap now limits study permits to roughly 485,000 annually. Post-graduation work permits have tightened. Spousal open work permits are restricted. The financial requirement has jumped. This route is still viable — but it is no longer the backup plan for those who cannot clear Express Entry.

The Cap
485,000 / year

Introduced in 2024, the cap allocates study permits by province and institution. Popular colleges in Ontario and British Columbia fill their quotas quickly. A letter of acceptance is no longer a guarantee of a permit.

Post-Graduation Work Permit
Tightened

You must graduate from a designated learning institution (DLI) to qualify. Not all programs at all DLIs qualify. Verify your program's PGWP eligibility before you pay tuition — not after.

Financial Requirement
$20,635 CAD / year

You must show proof of funds covering roughly $20,635 CAD per year for living expenses — tuition is additional. This is a hard floor, not a suggestion. Officers routinely refuse applicants who meet the academic criteria but fall short on funds.

What I Wish I Knew at 1am

The "study in Canada, get PR" narrative skips the part where you need Canadian work experience to compete in Express Entry. A PGWP gives you that window — typically 1–3 years. Use it strategically. Aim for NOC 0, A, or B work. A cashier job does not build your CRS.

Money

Cost of Living Reality

Canada's housing crisis is not abstract. In Toronto and Vancouver, a one-bedroom apartment consumes nearly half of a mid-level engineer's after-tax income. Smaller cities offer relief, but they come with fewer jobs and colder winters. The math is brutal and it is non-negotiable.

The Provincial Tax Trap

Alberta has no provincial sales tax and lower income tax. Quebec has higher taxes but subsidized childcare. Ontario sits in the middle. Your "salary" is not your salary. Run the numbers for the specific province before you accept a job offer.

Work

Credential Recognition

A Nigerian engineering degree is generally recognized at face value by Canadian employers. But "recognized" and "licensed to practice" are different things. Professional engineering is regulated provincially, and the path to a P.Eng. designation often requires Canadian work experience, exams, and additional coursework.

Engineering Licensing
Province-Specific

Each province has its own regulator — Professional Engineers Ontario (PEO), Engineers Geoscientists British Columbia (EGBC), Association of Professional Engineers and Geoscientists of Alberta (APEGA). The process typically involves an academic assessment, professional practice exam, and 48 months of acceptable engineering experience (some of which can be foreign).

The Bridging Gap
Plan for 2–4 Years

Many Nigerian engineers work in "engineer-in-training" or technologist roles while completing licensing requirements. This is normal. What is not normal is arriving in Canada expecting to walk into a senior engineering role without understanding this timeline.

The Hard Truth

The Catch

Every country profile on JapaGrid has a catch. This is Canada's.

Canada is not "easy" anymore.

The post-2024 policy changes have stripped away the shortcuts. Express Entry scores are punishing without a category draw or PNP. The study permit cap means competition for limited spots. Housing in the cities where the jobs are is a genuine crisis. Credential recognition is a multi-year project, not a formality. And the winters — the ones people joke about — are physically and mentally real, especially for newcomers from tropical climates.

Canada still works. People still arrive, build lives, and thrive. But the path is narrower, slower, and more expensive than the 2019 narrative suggested. If you are coming, come with accurate information, sufficient funds, and a timeline measured in years, not months.

Reality Check

Do not move to Toronto or Vancouver without a job offer or significant savings. The rental market is hostile, landlords demand proof of Canadian income, and Airbnb prices will destroy your runway in weeks. Consider landing in a smaller city, securing employment, and relocating later if necessary.
